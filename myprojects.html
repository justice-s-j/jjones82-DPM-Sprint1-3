Iowa City Bike Library Engagement Survey Case

Worked with a small team to analyze survey data for the Iowa City Bike Library, focusing on awareness, engagement, and interest across different age groups, genders, and University of Iowa affiliation. We used Excel, pivot tables, chi-square tests, residual analysis, and ANOVA to identify which services and communication channels were most effective for each segment, then summarized key findings and marketing recommendations for the organization.

Cubs Naming Rights Valuation Analysis

This project analyzed what drives stadium naming rights revenue in Major League Baseball and estimated a fair naming rights value for the Chicago Cubs. Using data on payroll, ticket sales, stadium capacity, population, win percentage, and franchise valuation, our team ran multiple regression tests to identify which factors actually predict naming-rights revenue. After removing weak predictors, total team payroll emerged as the strongest and most statistically significant variable. The final model (R² ≈ 0.52) showed that higher payroll is associated with higher naming-rights deals, allowing us to estimate a market value of approximately $6.86 million per year for the Cubs.

Predicting NFL Passer Rating

This was a sophomore-year project for my Foundations of Business Analytics course. Working with a team, I used data from 50 NFL quarterbacks and built a multiple regression model to see how stats like completion percentage, yards per attempt, touchdown percentage, and interception percentage relate to passer rating. It was a fairly simple project, but it gave me an early look at what’s possible with data and statistical modeling.
